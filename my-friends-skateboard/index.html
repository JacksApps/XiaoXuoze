The day she departed, Shailene left behind a piece of herself in Sydney: a short black stubby skateboard, with four green wheels still with faint traces of dirt left from her adventures.

Sweet memories can be reminders of the good ol’ days gone by, but wistful feelings can’t bring the future.

Despite our sincerity, farewells and promises gradually erode away with each passing season. Even treasured gifts placed high on our bookshelves eventually become just another dormant piece, part of the room's décor.

I remember the day she handed me the skateboard. I paused when I accepted, as it as if it were a family heirloom being passed down. Without speaking, the look in her eyes told me her last request:

"Be free and have fun, as I did."

All I could return was a nod; it wasn't a promise I could fulfil at that moment, but one I could only prove by living the rest of my life.

My trusty four-wheeled stallion always leads me to new places and brings the freeing wind along to help me find my own sense of freedom.

Months after, my friend Shailene’s voice grows fainter. But the skateboard she left is always there resting on my balcony, nudging me to go out for another ride. Reminding me to keep on living and never let the wheels come to a stop.

Months have passed since my friend Shailene left, and my memory of lively spirit grows fainter. Yet, the skateboard she left behind is always there waiting on my balcony, a silent nudge for me to go out for another ride.

I only need to 兜风 to remember her spirit lives on. To embrace the wind on my face, and to keep exploring.

And just like the wheels of my friend's board, I'll never let my spirit come to a stop.
